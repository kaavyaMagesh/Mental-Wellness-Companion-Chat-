## Overview
Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) is an approach that focuses on the connection between thoughts, emotions, and actions. Many CBT-based resources teach practical skills for recognizing unhelpful thinking patterns and responding to challenges in more constructive ways.

## Key Ideas
- Thoughts, feelings, and behaviors influence one another.
- Awareness of thinking patterns can support better decision-making.
- Skills improve through regular practice.
- Small, consistent changes often lead to meaningful progress over time.

## Common Skills
- Identifying unhelpful thoughts
- Reframing situations from different perspectives
- Problem-solving
- Goal setting
- Reflection and journaling
