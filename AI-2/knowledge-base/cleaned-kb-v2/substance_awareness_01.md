# Substance Awareness: Use, Misuse, and Warning Signs

## Overview
Substance awareness starts with understanding the difference between use, misuse, and harmful patterns. Not all substance use becomes a disorder, but any use can become risky depending on frequency, amount, context, and impact on daily life. Early awareness helps people notice changes before the problem becomes more serious. It also makes conversations about safety and support easier to begin.

## Key Concepts / Symptoms
Warning signs may include using more than intended, needing substances to cope with stress, or having trouble cutting back. Other signs can include changes in mood, sleep, energy, work, or relationships. People may hide use, lose interest in usual activities, or take more risks. These changes can happen gradually, which is why they are easy to miss at first.

## Coping Strategies
Pay attention to patterns, triggers, and reasons for use. Keep track of whether substances are starting to replace healthier coping habits. If someone is concerned, honest self-reflection and trusted conversations can help. Supportive approaches work better than blame or shame.

## When to Seek Support
Seek help if substance use is affecting health, safety, work, or relationships. Professional support can help a person understand their pattern and decide what level of change or treatment is needed.
