# Sleep Disorders: Common Types and Basics

## Overview
Sleep disorders are conditions that affect the quality, timing, or amount of sleep and can disrupt daytime functioning. They can make it harder to fall asleep, stay asleep, wake up refreshed, or feel alert during the day. Some sleep problems are temporary, while others are ongoing and may need medical care. Understanding the basics is an important first step toward better sleep and better health.

## Key Concepts / Symptoms
Common sleep disorders include insomnia, sleep apnea, restless legs syndrome, and narcolepsy. Symptoms may include trouble falling asleep, frequent waking, snoring, daytime sleepiness, or difficulty staying awake at the right times. People may also feel irritable, foggy, or less focused. Sleep problems can affect both mental and physical well-being.

## Coping Strategies
Start by noticing patterns in sleep timing, environment, and daily habits. Keeping a sleep diary can help identify triggers or routines that may be affecting rest. Simple sleep hygiene habits, such as a consistent bedtime and a calm evening routine, can support better sleep. If symptoms persist, medical evaluation may be needed to identify the type of sleep disorder.

## When to Seek Support
Seek professional evaluation if sleep problems are frequent, long-lasting, or affecting daytime life. Loud snoring, breathing pauses, severe fatigue, or sudden sleep attacks are especially important to discuss with a clinician.
