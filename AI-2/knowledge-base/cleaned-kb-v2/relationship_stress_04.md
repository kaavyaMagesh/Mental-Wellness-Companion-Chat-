# Relationship Stress: Daily Check-In Habits

## Overview
Small daily check-ins can help couples or close partners stay connected during stressful periods. These conversations do not need to be long. A few intentional minutes can help both people feel seen, informed, and supported. Over time, regular check-ins can reduce the buildup of tension and make bigger conversations easier.

## Key Concepts / Symptoms
When couples stop checking in, they may begin making assumptions about each other’s feelings or needs. Stress can make both people focus on survival mode instead of connection. This often leads to emotional distance, missed signals, or frustration over unspoken expectations. A brief daily check-in can interrupt this pattern before it grows.

## Coping Strategies
Ask simple questions like “How are you doing today?” or “What do you need from me right now?” Share one stressor and one need without trying to solve everything at once. Some couples find it helpful to check in at the end of the day, while others prefer mornings or weekends. The goal is consistency, not perfection.

## When to Seek Support
If daily check-ins still leave both people feeling disconnected or misunderstood, a therapist may help. Structured support can make communication more effective and reduce repeated stress patterns.
