# Grief Support: Self-Care During Loss

## Overview
Self-care during grief means supporting the body and mind with small, steady habits. Grief can make everything feel harder, including eating, sleeping, concentrating, and making decisions. In the middle of loss, self-care does not need to be elaborate. Simple routines and gentle support often matter most. Even a few basic habits can help create stability during an emotionally difficult time.

## Key Concepts / Symptoms
Grief can affect physical health as well as emotions. People may feel tired, tense, forgetful, or disconnected from daily tasks. Meals may be skipped, sleep may become irregular, and motivation may drop. These changes are common, but they can become more difficult if stress continues for a long time. Recognizing these shifts early can help someone respond with care instead of self-judgment.

## Coping Strategies
Try to keep meals and hydration simple and regular. Protect sleep by reducing late-night screen time and creating a calming bedtime routine. Gentle movement, breathing exercises, short walks, and quiet time can help the nervous system settle. It can also help to reduce extra obligations, ask for practical support, and focus on one day at a time.

## When to Seek Support
If grief is making it difficult to eat, sleep, work, or care for yourself, additional support may be useful. A counselor, therapist, or support group can help build routines and offer guidance during a painful period.
