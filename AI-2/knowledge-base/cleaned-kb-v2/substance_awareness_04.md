# Substance Awareness: Talking With Care

## Overview
Respectful conversations can make it easier to discuss substance use and offer support. Many people avoid the topic because they worry about causing conflict or sounding judgmental. A calm and caring approach increases the chance that the other person will stay engaged. The goal is not to win an argument but to open a useful conversation.

## Key Concepts / Symptoms
People may become defensive if they feel blamed, shamed, or controlled. They may also deny a problem even when there are clear warning signs. This is often tied to fear, embarrassment, or lack of readiness to change. A careful conversation should focus on concern, safety, and support rather than labels.

## Coping Strategies
Start gently and speak about specific behaviors or concerns you have noticed. Use calm language, avoid lecturing, and listen without interrupting. Ask what kind of support might be helpful and offer practical next steps. If the person is open to it, help them connect with a counselor, doctor, or support service.

## When to Seek Support
If substance use is creating danger, severe conflict, or health problems, outside support may be necessary. Immediate help is important if someone appears intoxicated to the point of risk or cannot stay safe.
