# Sleep Disorders: Sleep Hygiene Habits

## Overview
Sleep hygiene refers to the habits and conditions that support restful sleep. While sleep hygiene does not fix every sleep disorder, it can improve consistency and reduce common disruptions. Good sleep habits are often the foundation for better rest. They can also make other treatments more effective when medical care is needed.

## Key Concepts / Symptoms
People with poor sleep hygiene may have irregular bedtimes, heavy screen use at night, too much caffeine, or a sleep environment that is bright, noisy, or uncomfortable. These patterns can make it harder for the brain and body to settle into sleep. Over time, inconsistent habits can make sleep problems worse even when a person is tired.

## Coping Strategies
Keep a regular wake time and bedtime as much as possible. Make the bedroom dark, quiet, and cool, and reduce screens before bed. Avoid caffeine late in the day and try to give yourself a wind-down period at night. A short, predictable bedtime routine can signal to the body that it is time to rest.

## When to Seek Support
If good sleep habits do not improve rest after several weeks, there may be an underlying sleep disorder. A clinician can help determine whether further evaluation or treatment is needed.
