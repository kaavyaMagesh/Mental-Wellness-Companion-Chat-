# Substance Awareness: Stress, Trauma, and Risk

## Overview
Stress and trauma can increase the risk of using substances to cope. For some people, substances may temporarily reduce emotional pain, numb distress, or help them escape difficult feelings. While this may bring short-term relief, it can create bigger problems over time. Understanding the connection between stress and substance use is important for prevention and support.

## Key Concepts / Symptoms
People under chronic stress may feel overwhelmed, anxious, tired, or disconnected from support. Trauma can also affect sleep, mood, and coping ability. In that state, substances may seem like a fast way to calm down or shut things off. Over time, this can lead to dependence on the substance instead of healthier coping tools.

## Coping Strategies
Healthier coping options include talking with someone trusted, using movement or breathing exercises, and creating a more stable routine. Supportive relationships, counseling, and stress reduction practices can lower the urge to use substances for relief. It also helps to identify the situations, feelings, or times of day that increase risk.

## When to Seek Support
If stress or trauma is driving substance use, professional support may help address both issues together. A counselor or treatment program can offer safer coping strategies and trauma-informed care.
