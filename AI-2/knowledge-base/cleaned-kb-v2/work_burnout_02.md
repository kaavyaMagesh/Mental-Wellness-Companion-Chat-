# Work Burnout: Daily Habits That Reduce Stress

## Overview
Daily habits can either add to burnout or help reduce it. While big changes may be needed in some cases, small routines often create the first signs of relief. The goal is to support energy, focus, and recovery throughout the day. Consistent habits can also make it easier to notice when stress is building again.

## Key Concepts / Symptoms
People under heavy work stress often skip meals, sit too long, stay connected after hours, or ignore fatigue. Over time, this can make burnout worse. The body and mind need regular recovery signals to stay balanced. Without them, stress can become a constant background state.

## Coping Strategies
Take short movement breaks during the day, even if they are only a few minutes long. Protect sleep by keeping a regular bedtime when possible. Eat at consistent times and avoid working through meals. If screens and notifications are constant, create windows for checking messages instead of being available all the time.

## When to Seek Support
If daily habits are not enough and stress keeps rising, it may be time to ask for help. A manager, therapist, or healthcare professional can help identify what needs to change.
