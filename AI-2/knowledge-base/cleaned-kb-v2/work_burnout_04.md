# Work Burnout: Recovery and Rebuilding

## Overview
Recovering from burnout usually takes time and a slower pace than people expect. The first step is often rest, but recovery also involves changing the habits or conditions that created the burnout. Without those changes, the same stress can quickly return. A thoughtful rebuild can help someone return to work more sustainably.

## Key Concepts / Symptoms
People recovering from burnout may still feel emotionally drained, mentally foggy, or easily overwhelmed. Motivation can be low, and even simple tasks may require more energy than before. These are normal signs that the system needs time and support. Recovery may move forward unevenly, with better days and harder days.

## Coping Strategies
Reduce demands where possible and rebuild routines gradually. Review what caused the burnout, including workload, boundaries, or lack of support. Add protective habits such as regular breaks, clearer communication, and more realistic expectations. If possible, return to high-pressure tasks slowly rather than all at once.

## When to Seek Support
If recovery is not moving forward or work stress is causing ongoing distress, professional help can be useful. A therapist, doctor, or career advisor may help create a safer and more sustainable plan.
